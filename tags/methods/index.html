Correlation Doesn't Even Imply ... Correlation, Necessarily

12 Sep 2018, 00:00
Correlation / Causation / Methods / R / p-value / ggplot2

There’s a problem with the various versions of the oft-heard-in-intro-stats-courses phrase “correlation does not imply causation.” It gives early statistics students the wrong impressions. The idea seems simple: Just because there’s statistical correlation in the present does not mean that that there is a real world causal relationship (a complicated term, it turns out) between x and y. For instance, x could have a high correlation with y but actually, w is simply a mutual cause of x and y.
